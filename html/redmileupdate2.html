About


Welcome to the ultimate fan hub for all things Calgary Flames! Whether you're a long-time supporter or a newcomer to the world of hockey, this site is your one-stop destination for everything related to the Flames- Canada's proud and fiery team.

Founded in 1972, the Calgary Flames have established themselves as one of the most passionate teams in the NHL. With a rich history full of memorable moments, from their first Stanley Cup championship in 1989 to their intense rivalries, the Flames have captured the hearts of fans both in Calgary and around the world.

From the iconic red jerseys to the electrifying atmosphere at the Scotiabank Saddledome, the Flames are more than just a hockey team; they are a symbol of community, determination, and pride. Whether it’s the roar of the crowd during a big win or the electric tension of a playoff battle, the Flames are a team that ignites excitement wherever they go.

Here, you'll find the latest news, in-depth player stats, game schedules, season highlights, and everything you need to celebrate your love for the Flames. Join us as we continue to cheer on our team in their pursuit of greatness on the ice.

Stay connected, and let’s bring the heat as we support the Calgary Flames to new heights!

Johnny Hockey


On August 29, 2024, the entire hockey world was stunned and extremely saddened to learn of the tragic passing of Johnny Gaudreau and his brother, Matthew. At Fire on Ice we are committed to keeping Johnny's legacy alive after everything he did for the city of Calgary. Legends live forever. Rest in peace, Johnny Hockey.





Biography
Johnny Gaudreau, often referred to as "Johnny Hockey," was a highly skilled professional ice hockey player known for his playmaking abilities and offensive creativity. Born on August 13, 1993, in Salem, New Jersey, Gaudreau grew up playing hockey in the United States, where he quickly became known for his agility, vision, and remarkable puck-handling skills. Gaudreau played his college hockey at Boston College, where he made a significant impact, earning multiple accolades, including the prestigious Hobey Baker Award as the best college player in 2014. His combination of speed, finesse, and scoring ability made him one of the most exciting young players to watch.

In 2014, Gaudreau made his NHL debut with the Calgary Flames, where he became a key figure in the team's offense. Over the years, he developed a reputation as one of the league’s most dynamic wingers, consistently among the top scorers. Gaudreau's ability to create scoring chances with his quick release and sharp playmaking vision earned him several All-Star appearances and led the Flames to several playoff berths. In 2022, Gaudreau made the decision to leave Calgary and signed with the Columbus Blue Jackets, where he continued to be a top offensive contributor. Despite his smaller stature, standing at 5'9" and weighing 165 pounds, Gaudreau's playstyle defied his size, as he used his quickness, high hockey IQ, and elusive movements to outmaneuver larger opponents and create scoring opportunities for himself and his teammates.

Johnny was and continues to be an inspiration to all players around the league, especially those who have been told they are too small to have success in the NHL. Johnny was beloved by his teammates, coaches, fans, and above all, his family. He brought the city of Calgary back to life and made it exciting to be a Flames fan again. Tragically, on August 29th of this year, Johnny and his brother Matthew were struck and killed by a drunk driver while riding bicycles in their home state of New Jersey. Johnny leaves behind a wife, two young children, and a child on the way. Johnny will always be remembered in Calgary for the joy that he brought to our city. Our deepest and most heartfelt condolences and prayers are extended to the entire Gaudreau family during this extremely difficult time.
